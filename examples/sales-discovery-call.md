# Sales example: discovery call

Rep: Thanks for joining. What made you take the meeting?
Prospect: We are trying to tighten handoffs between sales, onboarding, and customer success.
Rep: What is happening today?
Prospect: Every team has a different version of the customer story.
Rep: Where does that show up?
Prospect: Onboarding starts without knowing the promised outcomes. Success finds out later and has to rebuild trust.
Rep: How are you solving it now?
Prospect: Notes in OneNote, CRM fields, and a weekly meeting.
Rep: What is not working about that?
Prospect: The notes are scattered. The CRM has fields, but not the reasoning behind decisions.
Rep: What would better look like?
Prospect: A shared account memory that captures decisions, risks, stakeholders, and next steps from calls.
Rep: Who cares most about this internally?
Prospect: Our VP of Customer Success is the decision-maker. Revenue operations will evaluate fit.
Rep: What timeline are you working against?
Prospect: We want something piloted before the Q3 planning cycle in August.
Rep: Is there budget attached?
Prospect: Yes. We have budget if it replaces manual account review prep.
Rep: What are the top pain points?
Prospect: First, repeated discovery. Second, missed commitments because nobody sees the original context.
Rep: What happens if this stays the same?
Prospect: We keep losing time before renewals and we look disorganized to strategic accounts.
Rep: What systems would this need to coexist with?
Prospect: CRM, shared docs, and Teams. It does not need to replace them.
Rep: Any concerns about a tool like this?
Prospect: Adoption. If reps have to do extra admin, it will fail.
Rep: What would make adoption easier?
Prospect: Paste a call transcript and get an account brief, follow-up email, and action items.
Rep: How will you judge a pilot?
Prospect: Faster account reviews and fewer follow-up questions from onboarding.
Rep: What is the next step?
Prospect: Send a two-page overview and a sample output from a discovery call. Then we can bring in revenue operations.
Rep: I'll send that by Friday and include a sample using anonymized notes.
Prospect: Perfect. Keep it practical. No long deck.
