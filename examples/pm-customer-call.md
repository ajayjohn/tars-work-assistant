# Product example: customer discovery call

00:00 Speaker 1: Thanks for making time. I'm Alex from the product team. Could you start with what your team is trying to improve this quarter?
00:18 Speaker 2: I'm Jordan. We run operations at Acme. Our biggest problem is handoffs between account managers and implementation.
00:41 Speaker 1: What breaks in the handoff?
01:02 Speaker 2: Context disappears. The account manager hears the customer's urgency, but implementation only sees a short form.
01:34 Speaker 1: How do you track that today?
01:50 Speaker 2: OneNote pages, a spreadsheet, and a few Teams threads. It works until the deal is complex.
02:22 Speaker 1: What would a better version look like?
02:45 Speaker 2: I want a launch workspace that pulls notes, risks, stakeholders, and open questions into one place.
03:12 Speaker 1: Is that more about search or workflow?
03:28 Speaker 2: Workflow. Search is table stakes. I need prompts that remind people what has not been decided.
04:10 Speaker 1: What tools have you tried?
04:27 Speaker 2: Northwind has something close. Their customer timeline is strong, but it feels locked to their CRM.
05:05 Speaker 1: What did you like about that timeline?
05:20 Speaker 2: It showed the real story. Meetings, decisions, risk notes, support tickets, all together.
06:01 Speaker 1: What stopped you from switching?
06:20 Speaker 2: Our implementation team lives in a different stack. I do not want another system nobody checks.
07:04 Speaker 1: How urgent is this problem?
07:21 Speaker 2: Pretty urgent. We have two renewals at risk because customers feel like they repeat themselves.
08:00 Speaker 1: What happens if this is not fixed?
08:17 Speaker 2: We lose confidence with strategic accounts. Maybe not this month, but by renewal review.
09:03 Speaker 1: What would make this a must-have?
09:28 Speaker 2: If it could capture decisions from calls and turn them into a clean internal brief.
10:02 Speaker 1: Who else would need to approve it?
10:19 Speaker 2: Sam in implementation and Taylor in revenue operations.
11:01 Speaker 1: What objections do you expect from them?
11:19 Speaker 2: Sam will worry about extra process. Taylor will ask whether it integrates with our reporting.
12:04 Speaker 1: If we built a first version, what should it include?
12:25 Speaker 2: Stakeholder map, decision log, open risks, and a follow-up draft after calls.
13:10 Speaker 1: What should it not include?
13:28 Speaker 2: No heavy project plan. We already have that. Keep it focused on account context.
14:08 Speaker 1: How would you judge success?
14:30 Speaker 2: Fewer repeated questions, faster kickoff, fewer surprises in renewal meetings.
15:14 Speaker 1: Any final advice?
15:32 Speaker 2: Do not make people maintain it manually. It has to work from what they already paste or discuss.
